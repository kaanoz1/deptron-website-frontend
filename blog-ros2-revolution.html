ROS2 Revolution in Industrial Robotics

As the world of robotics moves from academic laboratories to factory floors, it is undergoing a major transformation in software infrastructure. This is where ROS2 changes the rules of the game.

Why ROS2 and not ROS1?

The first Robot Operating System (ROS) was an excellent tool for accelerating robotics research. However, industrial production lines demand more than just "acceleration": Uninterrupted Safety and Real-Time Performance.

As Deptron Robotics, all systems we develop are built on ROS2 (Robot Operating System 2). Because there is no room for error in the chaotic environment of a modern warehouse.

3 Critical Factors for Industrial Reliability

There are three main technical reasons behind our transition to ROS2:

  • 1. Real-Time Data Processing:
    In legacy systems, data packets could sometimes be delayed. Thanks to ROS2 and the DDS (Data Distribution Service) layer, "obstacle ahead" data from a robot's sensor is transmitted to the motors as a "stop" command within milliseconds. This is vital for occupational safety.
  • 2. Decentralized Architecture:
    In ROS1, if the "Master Node" crashed, the entire system would stop. In ROS2, there is no central node. Even if the robot's navigation system fails, safety sensors continue to work. This increases the overall resilience of the system.
  • 3. Cyber Security:
    Digitalization of factories brings cyber risks. ROS2 supports industrial-standard encryption and authentication protocols, protecting your robots against external interference.
